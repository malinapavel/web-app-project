WHO ARE WE?



WeirdoWorld is a team of journalists who want to deliver intriguing news, be it tragic or funny. We consider that the news we provide to you are escaping from the austerity nature of journalism, whilst still keeping objectivity to the fullest.

Mind you, we are not trying to mock any individual participating in strange incidents, but rather we ilustrate the weirdly funny course of events.

CODE OF DEONTOLOGY



Prior Considerations

This ethical code is situated in the context created by the existing regulatory framework, namely the audiovisual law, the audiovisual code, the civil code, the European Convention on Human Rights, the ECHR jurisprudence.

These regulations are assumed as rules of editorial conduct, both by virtue of their character of legal obligation, and with the conviction that the public interest mission of broadcasters must be built in the matter offered by audiovisual communication carried out in compliance with the law.



Level of self-regulation

This code does not duplicate the existing regulations, but assumes them as external reference elements for the addition of additional rules or principles, which in fact constitute its subject matter. For this reason and to avoid the inconsistency of reproducing in the field of self-regulation provisions that are directly mandatory by law or secondary legislation and cannot be assumed as optional, even the most important of the legal regulations will not be found in its content.



Principles

This code values the conviction that the obligation to comply with the law and regulations can coexist without contradicting the need that broadcasters, producers, publishers and editors have to use the values that emerge from experience, common sense, editorial and ethical benchmarks, when faced with editorial challenges or when carrying out the routine of ordinary editorial activities. "In a perfect world only one rule would work: use your own and best judgment!" (BBC Editorial Guidelines)

The trust of the public is another value that we keep as a benchmark and it can only be won through impartiality and honesty and by guaranteeing that the public is avoided at all times to be misled or manipulated.

The accuracy of the presentation of the truth cannot be achieved only by simply presenting the facts, but only by an effort to detect the relevant aspects, to distinguish the evidence from prejudice or confusion to benefit from the uncertainty function, and to discover the truth where it lies (BBC-EG)

Editorial integrity and independence must be preserved in the face of any external interests, any political or commercial pressures and any personal interests. (BBC-EG)

The editorial content must provide useful meaning for the audience, not just stimulate reactions or make them the target of the advertising message. Entertainment also offers this type of meaning in the form of value constructed with humor, good sense, and good inspiration.



Value judgements/opinions

Although opinions are necessarily subjective and, consequently, cannot and should not be subject to criteria of correctness (as expressed by the ECtHR, "value judgments should not be subject to the test of veracity"), there must still be an assurance that the opinions are honestly and ethically expressed and that they are based on credible and sufficient factual support.

Opinions that take the form of comments on events or actions regarding persons or institutions must not try to deny the reality of facts or data.

In the exercise of the editorial activity, both the legitimate and mutual respect for the ideological orientations of the editors and the owners must be shown, but also the acceptance of the fact that this editorial activity is limited by demanding imperatives consisting in the reporting of correct news and ethical opinions. This is essential in order to respect the citizens' fundamental right to information. (Rez COE 1003/93)
